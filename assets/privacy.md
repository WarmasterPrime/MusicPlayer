# Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how Virtma ("we", "us", or "our"), the parent company of DOFT, collects, uses, and shares your personal information.

### 1. Information We Collect
When you create an account or access our services, we may collect the following information:
- Username
- First Name and Last Name
- Email Address
- Phone Number
- Profile Information (e.g., profile picture, background preferences)
- Password
- Google Account Information (Full name, Email, Phone number).
- Public IP Address (This is the IP address associated with your router that all websites receive upon connecting and accessing pages).
- You location (Country, State/Region, City, Zip/Postal Code)
- Browser Information (e.g., Chrome, Firefox, Safari)
- Operating System Information (e.g., Windows, macOS, Linux)
- Device Information (e.g., Desktop, Laptop, Tablet)
- Device Model Information (e.g., MacBook Pro, Dell XPS 15)
- Device Manufacturer Information (e.g., Apple, Dell, HP)
- Files uploaded (These are files that you have to manually specify when uploading content to our services)
- Your local time zone (e.g., UTC, GMT, EST)
- Your language preference (e.g., English, Spanish, French)
- Network connectivity information (e.g., upload speed, download speed, ping time)
- Your browser's user agent string (This is a unique identifier that your browser sends to servers when you visit a website, which can include information about your operating system, browser version, and hardware specifications).
- Your browser's cookie information (This is a unique identifier that your browser sends to servers when you visit a website, which can include information about your browsing history, preferences, and other data).
- How you use our products (e.g., how often you visit our website, how long you spend on each page, etc.).
- Your preferences (e.g., language preference, time zone, etc.).
- Items you create using our services (e.g., playlists, albums, templates, designs, songs, etc.).

### 2. How We Use Your Information
Your information is used to:
- Provide and maintain our services.
- Personalize your experience.
- Communicate with you regarding your account.
- Facilitate cross-project access within the Virtma ecosystem (including DOFT and other Virtma-created projects).
- Diagnostic information.
- Analytics information.
- Marketing (Information may be sold unless requested otherwise).
- Customer support.
- Research and development.
- Security purposes.

### 3. Data Access and Sharing
Your account data may be accessed and used by other projects created by Virtma to provide a unified experience across our platforms.
You have the right to request all of the information that we collect about you. You can download your data by viewing your profile and selecting "Download My Data" from the options listed. If none are displayed, please contact us at [dan.k.valente@proton.me](mailto:dan.k.valente@proton.me) and include in your subject line "Request My Data".

### 4. Updates to this Policy
This Privacy Policy may be updated at any time without prior notice. Your continued use of the service constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

### 5. Cookies
We use cookies to store information about your browsing behavior and preferences. Cookies are small files that are stored on your device by the website you visit. We use cookies to provide a personalized experience, track your activity, and analyze our services.
We may use third-party cookies to provide additional services or to analyze user behavior.
We may use cookies to store information about your preferences, such as your language preference or your time zone.
We may use cookies to store information about your browsing history, such as the pages you have visited.
We may use cookies to store information about your device, such as the type of device you are using.
Some things that we may store cookies for include (But are not limited to):
- Personalized content
- Tracking your activity (e.g., page views, clicks, scrolls, play history, etc.)
- Analyzing user behavior
- Providing additional services
- Research and development
- Security purposes
